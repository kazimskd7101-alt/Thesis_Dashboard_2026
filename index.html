Operational Interpretation

How the artifact should be used in practice

Recommended use

Use the dashboard as a triage tool. Surface only extreme-score cases to the analyst or trader and abstain elsewhere.

Do not use as

A universal always-on intraday trading engine. Full-sample results are bounded and the strongest evidence is selective, not universal.

Best hierarchy

  • Best broad-coverage model: Single I60 OHLC-only
  • Best strict selective finalist: Single I120 OHLC-only
  • Best classical visual comparator: HOG
  • Weak classical comparator: HAAR-like

Artifact Governance Logic

How the system should be controlled in a real setting

Step 01

Chronology-safe engineering

Build the front-contract series carefully and never mix contract chronology carelessly across sparse deferred maturities.

Step 02

Benchmark before trust

Compare CNN outputs against numeric, directional, and classical visual baselines before elevating any model for operational use.

Step 03

Selective escalation

Surface only the most extreme-score cases to users and abstain on the large ambiguous interior of the score distribution.

Step 04

XAI as inspection

Use overlays to inspect chart-focused versus suspiciously background-heavy cases, not as proof of true economic reasoning.

Step 05

Human-in-the-loop use

The artifact should support analyst judgment, not replace it.
